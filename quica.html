Interactive Installation
Physics in Everyday Life

Celebrating the 201st anniversary of the National Museum with immersive, educational interaction.

About the Project

An interactive installation by Ambos&&, produced for an event at Sesc Tijuca celebrating the 201st anniversary of the National Museum, with the theme “Physics in Everyday Life.”

How It Works

The installation uses Processing and Kinect technology to create an engaging experience: several balls fall on the silhouettes of visitors, behaving according to physical properties like elasticity and weight. Each ball displays arrows indicating force and speed, turning the interaction into a playful and educational exploration of physics principles.

Impact

The installation successfully combined art, technology, and education, allowing visitors to intuitively understand physics principles through interactive play, making science more accessible and enjoyable.

Team

Ambos&&: Bárbara Castro, Jacqueline Pires, Júlia Fonseca, and Maria Clara Ruback.
